Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel

Introduction

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel was an Indian politician who played a pivotal role in the country's struggle for independence from British rule. He was also known as the "Iron Man of India" for his uncompromising will and determination.

Patel was born on 31 October 1875 in Nadiad, a small village in present-day Gujarat. He studied law in England and returned to India in 1913 to start his legal practice.

Legacy

Patel is remembered for his crucial role in the integration of India after independence. He worked tirelessly to unite the various princely states and create a single, unified nation. His vision and leadership helped shape modern India.

Patel passed away on 15 December 1950, but his legacy lives on. He is revered as a national hero and his contributions to India's freedom struggle and nation-building continue to inspire generations.
